# The Fundamental Science Debate
# Voices: Fox Mulder narrates, Walter White and Richard Feynman debate

[voices]
FOX = fox-mulder.wav chunk_words=20
WALT = walt-danger.wav chunk_words=20 lpf=2500
FEYNMAN = feynman.wav chunk_words=30

[dialog]
FOX: [exaggeration=0.3 seed=5 temp=0.6 chunk_words=20]
    What you're about to hear is a conversation that was never
    supposed to happen. Two brilliant minds, each convinced their
    discipline holds the key to reality itself. But ask yourself: who
    decided there had to be only one answer?

WALT: [exaggeration=0.4 seed=5 temp=0.6 chunk_words=15 token_scale=1.2]
    Let me be perfectly clear. Chemistry is the study of matter, and matter
    is what everything is made of. You want to understand life? Disease?
    Explosions? It all comes down to chemistry.

FEYNMAN:
    Oh sure, chemistry is wonderful, I love chemistry! But you see, when
    you really get down to it, your chemicals are just atoms, and atoms are
    just physics. You're doing physics and calling it something else!

WALT: [exaggeration=0.3 seed=5 temp=0.6]
    That's reductive and you know it. When I synthesize a compound, I'm not
    solving Schrodinger's equation. I'm working with emergent properties that
    physics alone cannot predict.

FEYNMAN: [exaggeration=0.5 seed=5 temp=0.6 token_scale=1.2]
    Cannot predict... Yet! That's the whole game. Everything your molecules
    do, they do because of the electromagnetic force and quantum mechanics.
    Chemistry is applied physics, whether you like it or not.

WALT: [exaggeration=0.6 seed=159 temp=0.7]
    Applied physics. You know what else is applied physics? A brick falling
    on your head. That doesn't make masonry a branch of physics.

FEYNMAN: [exaggeration=0.7 seed=259 temp=0.8]
    Well actually it kind of does! Look, I'm not trying to insult
    chemistry. I'm saying there's a hierarchy, and at the bottom of that
    hierarchy, holding everything up, is physics.

WALT: [exaggeration=0.3 seed=5 temp=0.6]
    At the bottom. Interesting choice of words. Because from where I stand,
    chemistry is where the real complexity lives. Physics gives you rules.
    Chemistry gives you the world.

FOX: [exaggeration=0.5 seed=420 temp=0.6 token_scale=1.5]
    And there it is. Two men, both so certain that their framework is the
    foundation of reality. But maybe the real conspiracy is the question
    itself. Who benefits from keeping these disciplines separate? What if
    the truth is that neither chemistry nor physics is fundamental? What if
    the real answer is something neither of them wants to look at? Something
    hidden, deliberately, from both of them. Think about it.
